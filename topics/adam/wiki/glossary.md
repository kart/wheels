# Glossary

- `theta`: The parameter vector being optimized.
- `f(theta)`: Objective or loss function.
- `f_t(theta)`: Stochastic objective realization at timestep `t`, often from a minibatch.
- `g_t`: Gradient of `f_t` with respect to parameters at timestep `t`.
- Gradient: A vector of partial derivatives; each entry says how the objective locally changes as one parameter changes.
- Stochastic gradient: A gradient computed from a noisy sample or minibatch rather than the full objective.
- First-order method: An optimization method using gradients, not second derivatives/Hessians.
- Moment: A statistic of a distribution. In this paper, the first moment is mean-like and the second raw moment is squared-value-like.
- Exponential moving average: A running average that gives recent observations larger weight and older observations exponentially smaller weight.
- `m_t`: Adam's first-moment moving average of gradients.
- `v_t`: Adam's second raw moment moving average of squared gradients.
- Bias correction: Dividing by `1 - beta^t` to correct the zero-initialization cold start of moving averages.
- `alpha`: Base stepsize or learning rate.
- `beta_1`: Decay rate for first-moment memory.
- `beta_2`: Decay rate for second raw moment memory.
- `epsilon`: Small positive stabilizer added to the denominator.
- Sparse gradient: A gradient where many coordinates are zero or nearly zero at a given step.
- Non-stationary objective: An objective whose gradient distribution changes over time.
- Regret: In online learning, cumulative loss relative to the best fixed choice in hindsight.
- AdaGrad: Adaptive gradient method that accumulates squared gradients; discussed as useful for sparse gradients.
- RMSProp: Method using an exponentially decayed squared-gradient average; discussed as useful for non-stationary settings.
- AdaMax: Adam variant based on an infinity norm.
